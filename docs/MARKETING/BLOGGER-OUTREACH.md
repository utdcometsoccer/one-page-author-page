# Blogger Outreach Strategy for Authors

## Overview

Book bloggers are influential voices in the literary community with dedicated, engaged audiences. A strategic blogger outreach program can significantly boost book visibility, generate reviews, and drive sales. This guide provides a comprehensive approach to engaging with book bloggers effectively.

## Understanding the Book Blogging Landscape

### Types of Book Bloggers

1. **Genre-Specific Bloggers**
   - Focus on specific genres (romance, mystery, fantasy, etc.)
   - Deep expertise in their niche
   - Highly engaged niche audiences
   - Best for targeting ideal readers

2. **General Book Review Bloggers**
   - Review across multiple genres
   - Broader audience reach
   - More selective about what they review
   - Good for literary fiction and crossover titles

3. **BookTube/BookTok Creators**
   - Video-based content (YouTube, TikTok)
   - Younger, highly engaged audiences
   - Visual, personality-driven reviews
   - Potential for viral reach

4. **Bookstagram Influencers**
   - Instagram-based book content
   - Beautiful aesthetic photography
   - Strong community engagement
   - Visual appeal of book covers matters

5. **Multi-Platform Bloggers**
   - Active on blog, social media, and video
   - Largest reach and influence
   - Most selective and professional
   - Best for established authors or major launches

### Blogger Influence Metrics

**Micro-Influencers (500-5,000 followers):**
- High engagement rates (5-10%)
- Niche audiences
- More accessible
- Great for debut authors
- Often accept self-published books

**Mid-Tier Bloggers (5,000-50,000 followers):**
- Balanced reach and engagement
- Established reputation
- More selective
- May charge for reviews
- Prefer traditionally published books

**Top-Tier Bloggers (50,000+ followers):**
- Massive reach
- Industry connections
- Very selective
- Often charge fees
- Can make or break launches
- Primarily work with publishers

## Building Your Blogger List

### Research Process

1. **Genre Research**
   - Search: "[Your Genre] book blogger"
   - Search: "best book blogs [Genre]"
   - Check Goodreads groups
   - Browse blog directories
   - Follow blogger recommendation chains

2. **Evaluate Potential Bloggers**
   - Do they review your genre?
   - What's their audience size?
   - How engaged is their audience?
   - What's their review style?
   - Do they accept self-published/indie books?
   - What's their submission process?
   - Do they charge fees?
   - How often do they post?

3. **Create Blogger Database**
   Track in spreadsheet:
   - Blogger name
   - Blog URL
   - Email address
   - Social media handles
   - Genre focus
   - Audience size
   - Review policy
   - Submission method
   - Contact date
   - Status (contacted, reviewing, reviewed)
   - Review link

**Social Media Search:**

## Outreach Strategy

### Preparation Before Outreach

1. **Read Their Content**
   - Understand their review style
   - Note books they've enjoyed
   - Identify review patterns
   - Respect their stated preferences

2. **Follow Review Guidelines**
   - Every blogger has submission policies
   - Follow their process exactly
   - Note lead times (often 2-6 months)
   - Respect genre restrictions
   - Check if they're open to submissions

3. **Prepare Materials**
   - Professional book description
   - High-quality cover image
   - Author bio and photo
   - Book details (genre, page count, formats)
   - Purchase links
   - Review copies ready (ARC, ebook, print)
   - Press kit or media kit

### Outreach Email Template

**Subject Line Options:**
- "Review Request: [Book Title] - [Genre]"
- "[Blogger Name], would you review my [Genre] novel?"
- "ARC Request: [Book Title] by [Author Name]"

**Email Body:**

```
Hi [Blogger Name],

I've been following your blog and really enjoyed your recent review of [Specific Book They Reviewed]. Your insight about [specific detail] resonated with me.

I'm reaching out because I think my [Genre] novel, [Book Title], might be a good fit for your blog. Here's a brief description:

[2-3 sentence compelling hook/description]

[Book Title] is a [length] [genre] novel featuring [key themes/elements that align with their interests]. It will be released on [Date] by [Publisher or "independently"].

Some key details:
- Genre: [Primary Genre]
- Subgenres/Tropes: [If applicable]
- Page Count: [Number]
- Available Formats: [Ebook, Print, Audio]
- Content Warnings: [If applicable]

Would you be interested in receiving an ARC (Advanced Review Copy) for consideration? I can send it in [Formats Available].

I understand you receive many requests and have a busy schedule. There's no obligation, and I respect whatever decision you make. If you're not accepting submissions right now or this isn't a fit, I completely understand.

Thank you for your time and for all you do to support authors and readers.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

P.S. You can learn more about me and my work at [Your Author Website]
```

### Follow-Up Protocol

**Timeline:**
- **Week 0**: Initial outreach
- **Week 2-3**: Gentle follow-up (only if no response)
- **After sending book**: No follow-up unless blogger requests it
- **Post-review**: Thank you message

**Follow-Up Email Example:**

```
Hi [Blogger Name],

I wanted to follow up on my email from [Date] about my book [Title]. I know you're busy, so just checking if you had a chance to consider my review request.

If you're not interested or not accepting submissions right now, no worries at all! I'd still love to stay connected and see your future reviews.

Thanks again for your time.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Important Rules:**
- Never send unsolicited review copies
- Don't follow up more than once
- Respect "no" or silence as an answer
- Never demand a review or timeline
- Don't send reminder emails after they accept

## Working with Bloggers

### Best Practices

1. **Professionalism**
   - Treat bloggers as professional partners
   - Respect their time and expertise
   - Meet deadlines they set
   - Provide materials in requested formats
   - Respond promptly to questions

2. **Clear Communication**
   - State expectations upfront
   - Clarify if review is voluntary
   - Mention if you're requesting specific timing
   - Be clear about formats available
   - Provide all necessary information initially

3. **Provide Quality Materials**
   - Professional cover image (high resolution)
   - Well-edited book copy
   - Formatted correctly for different devices
   - Author photo (if requested)
   - Clear book description and metadata

4. **No Strings Attached**
   - Don't require positive reviews
   - Accept honest opinions gracefully
   - Don't ask to approve reviews before posting
   - Don't offer payment for positive reviews
   - Reviews should be freely given opinion

### Sending Review Copies

**Digital ARCs:**
- PDF (universally compatible)
- EPUB (preferred for ereaders)
- MOBI (for Kindle)
- Send via: Email, BookFunnel, Dropbox, Google Drive

**Physical ARCs:**
- Professional quality printing
- Include note card with publication info
- Send with tracking
- Budget for shipping costs
- Consider international shipping costs

**Advance Reader Copy (ARC) Note Example:**

```
Dear [Blogger Name],

Thank you for agreeing to review [Book Title]! Here's your digital ARC.

Book Details:
- Title: [Book Title]
- Author: [Your Name]
- Genre: [Genre]
- Publication Date: [Date]
- Publisher: [Publisher Name or "Independently Published"]

I would be grateful if you could post your review by [Date], but I understand if that timeline doesn't work. Please review honestly - I value authentic opinions.

Feel free to share your review on Amazon, Goodreads, your blog, and social media. Here are the links:
- Amazon: [Link]
- Goodreads: [Link]
- My Website: [Link]

Thank you so much for your time and support!

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Contact Email]
```

### After the Review

**When Review is Positive:**
- Send heartfelt thank you
- Share on social media (tag the blogger)
- Comment on their blog post
- Link to review from your website
- Include quote in marketing materials (with permission)
- Add to testimonials

**When Review is Mixed/Negative:**
- Thank them professionally
- Don't argue or defend
- Don't ask them to change it
- Don't post passive-aggressive responses
- Learn from constructive criticism
- Move on gracefully

**Thank You Email Template:**

```
Hi [Blogger Name],

Thank you so much for reading and reviewing [Book Title]! I really appreciate the time you took to share your thoughts with your readers.

I particularly loved your point about [specific detail from review]. It means a lot to know that [aspect of book] resonated with you.

I've shared your review on social media and will link to it from my website. Thank you again for supporting authors and connecting readers with books they'll love.

Best wishes,
[Your Name]
```

## Blogger Relationship Building

### Long-Term Engagement

1. **Stay Connected**
   - Follow on social media
   - Engage with their content regularly
   - Comment on blog posts (not just yours)
   - Share their reviews of other books
   - Support their work genuinely

2. **Add Value**
   - Share their content with your audience
   - Participate in blog tours
   - Offer guest posts
   - Do author interviews
   - Provide exclusive content
   - Host giveaways on their blog

3. **Build Relationships, Not Transactions**
   - Remember personal details
   - Support their favorite causes
   - Celebrate their milestones
   - Don't only contact when you need something
   - Be a genuine fan of their work

4. **Reciprocate**
   - Feature them on your blog/newsletter
   - Review books they recommend
   - Invite to beta read
   - Offer advanced looks at new projects
   - Provide exclusive content

### Blogger Events and Opportunities

**Blog Tours:**
- Coordinated review campaign
- Multiple bloggers, scheduled posts
- Mix of reviews, interviews, guest posts
- Usually organized by tour companies
- Cost: $50-500 depending on tour size

**Cover Reveals:**
- Exciting visual content
- Easy for bloggers (low time commitment)
- Good for building pre-launch buzz
- Provide graphics and text
- Coordinate timing across bloggers

**Author Interviews:**
- Q&A format
- Provides depth beyond book review
- Builds author platform
- Great content for blogger
- Good for author-reader connection

**Guest Posts:**
- Author writes content for blog
- Writing tips, behind-the-scenes, essays
- Provides value to blogger's audience
- Includes book promotion
- Win-win content arrangement

**Giveaways:**
- Author provides prize (signed books, swag)
- Blogger hosts contest on their site
- Drives engagement for blogger
- Increases awareness for author
- Builds email list (if entry requires signup)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Mass Generic Emails**: Personalize every outreach
2. **Not Reading Policies**: Follow each blogger's guidelines exactly
3. **Sending Unsolicited Books**: Always ask first
4. **Wrong Genre Targeting**: Only contact genre-appropriate bloggers
5. **Demanding Positive Reviews**: Accept honest opinions
6. **Too Short Notice**: Contact 2-6 months before release
7. **Arguing with Reviews**: Never dispute or defend against criticism
8. **No Follow-Through**: Send promised materials promptly
9. **Transactional Approach**: Build relationships, not just get reviews
10. **Ignoring Small Bloggers**: Micro-influencers often have better engagement

## Ethical Considerations

### Review Ethics

**Do:**
- Disclose that you provided free copy
- Accept honest reviews
- Be transparent about any relationships
- Follow FTC guidelines
- Respect editorial independence

**Don't:**
- Pay for positive reviews
- Require positive reviews
- Ask to approve reviews before posting
- Manipulate review content
- Create fake reviews
- Harass bloggers for reviews

### FTC Compliance

- Bloggers must disclose free products received
- Material connections must be disclosed
- Paid reviews must be clearly marked
- Both author and blogger share responsibility
- Violation can result in fines
