# Media Outreach Plan: Podcasts, YouTube, and Traditional Media

## Overview

Media appearances provide authors with opportunities to reach new audiences, establish credibility, and create evergreen promotional content. This comprehensive guide covers outreach to podcasters, YouTubers, and traditional media (TV, radio, newspapers).

---

## Part 1: Podcast Outreach

### The Podcast Landscape for Authors

**Why Podcasts Matter:**
- 464 million podcast listeners globally (2024)
- Average listener subscribes to 7 shows
- 71% of listeners take action after hearing ads
- Long-form format allows deep conversation
- Episodes remain accessible long-term
- Highly engaged, loyal audiences

**Types of Podcasts for Authors:**

1. **Book-Focused Podcasts**
   - Book reviews and recommendations
   - Author interviews
   - Genre-specific shows
   - Best for: All authors

2. **Writing & Publishing Podcasts**
   - Writing craft discussions
   - Publishing industry insights
   - Author career advice
   - Best for: Authors who also teach/coach

3. **Topic-Based Podcasts**
   - Shows about your book's subject matter
   - Industry or niche-specific content
   - Best for: Non-fiction authors, specialists

4. **Story-Focused Podcasts**
   - Narrative storytelling
   - Audio fiction
   - Best for: Fiction authors, storytellers

5. **General Interest Podcasts**
   - Interviews with interesting people
   - Wide variety of topics
   - Best for: Authors with compelling personal stories

### Finding Relevant Podcasts

**Discovery Methods:**

1. **Podcast Directories:**
   - Apple Podcasts
   - Spotify
   - Podchaser
   - Listen Notes
   - Chartable

2. **Search Strategies:**
   - "[Your Genre] podcast"
   - "Author interview podcast"
   - "[Your Book's Topic] podcast"
   - "[Your Profession/Expertise] podcast"

3. **Podcast Databases:**
   - Podmatch (connects guests with hosts)
   - PodcastGuests.com
   - Interview Valet
   - MatchMaker.fm

4. **Organic Research:**
   - Check what podcasts similar authors appeared on
   - Search "podcast" on author websites
   - Follow podcast recommendation chains
   - Join Facebook groups for podcast guests

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Episode frequency (active shows)
- Download numbers (if public)
- Guest profile (do they interview authors?)
- Topic fit with your book
- Audience demographics
- Production quality
- Review ratings and engagement

### Podcast Outreach Process

**Preparation:**

1. **Listen First**
   - Listen to 3-5 recent episodes
   - Understand the format
   - Note interviewer style
   - Identify audience interests
   - Find conversation angles

2. **Prepare Your Pitch**
   - Why you're a good fit for THIS show
   - Specific topic angles you can discuss
   - Your unique perspective or story
   - Value you'll provide to their audience
   - Your credentials/expertise

3. **Create a Media Kit**
   - Professional headshot
   - Bio (short and long versions)
   - Book description
   - Sample interview questions
   - Previous media appearances
   - Audio sample (if available)
   - Book cover image
   - Contact information

**Outreach Email Template:**

```
Subject: Guest Idea for [Podcast Name]: [Compelling Topic]

Hi [Host Name],

I've been listening to [Podcast Name] and really enjoyed your recent episode with [Guest Name] about [Topic]. Your question about [specific detail] was particularly insightful.

I'm reaching out because I think I could provide value to your audience with a conversation about [Specific Topic That Fits Their Show]. As [your credentials/expertise], I have a unique perspective on [topic] that aligns well with your show's focus on [their theme].

Some potential talking points:
• [Specific, interesting angle #1]
• [Specific, interesting angle #2]
• [Specific, interesting angle #3]

I'm the author of [Book Title], a [genre] book about [brief, compelling description]. But more importantly, I can speak to [broader topics beyond just your book].

You can hear my interview style on these podcasts:
- [Previous Appearance #1]
- [Previous Appearance #2]

Would you be interested in having me on the show? I'm flexible with scheduling and can record remotely with professional audio equipment.

My media kit with more details is attached. Thanks for considering, and keep up the great work with the show!

Best,
[Your Name]
[Website]
[Contact Info]
```

**Follow-Up Strategy:**
- Wait 1-2 weeks before following up
- Send one polite follow-up email
- Accept non-responses as a no
- Stay professional and gracious

### Preparing for the Interview

**Technical Setup:**
- Good quality microphone (Blue Yeti or similar)
- Quiet recording space
- Reliable internet connection
- Headphones (to avoid echo)
- Backup recording device
- Test equipment before call

**Content Preparation:**
- Review your talking points
- Prepare 3-5 compelling stories
- Have book details handy
- Know your key messages
- Prepare questions to ask host
- Practice succinct answers
- Prepare for common questions

**Common Interview Questions:**
- Tell us about your book
- What inspired you to write it?
- Who should read this book?
- What was your writing process?
- What's your background?
- What's next for you?
- Where can people find you?

**Interview Best Practices:**
- Be conversational and authentic
- Tell stories, not summaries
- Speak in soundbites
- Listen actively
- Let conversation flow naturally
- Mention your book naturally (don't force it)
- Provide value first, promote second
- Be enthusiastic but not sales-y
- Respect the host's time and format

### Post-Interview

**Thank You Process:**
- Send thank you email within 24 hours
- Express gratitude for the opportunity
- Mention specific moments you enjoyed
- Offer to return as guest anytime
- Stay connected on social media

**Promotion Plan:**
- Share episode when it releases
- Tag the podcast on social media
- Send to your email list
- Include on your website
- Create social media graphics
- Write blog post about appearance
- Use audio clips for promotion
- Thank the host publicly

**Sample Thank You Email:**

```
Hi [Host Name],

Thank you so much for having me on [Podcast Name] today! I really enjoyed our conversation, especially when we discussed [specific moment]. Your questions really helped me articulate [topic] in a new way.

Please let me know when the episode goes live—I'll be sure to share it with my audience. I'd love to return anytime you need a guest on [related topics].

Thanks again for what you do for authors and your listeners!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Part 2: YouTube Outreach

### YouTube for Authors

**Why YouTube Matters:**
- 2+ billion users worldwide
- Second largest search engine (after Google)
- Video content is highly engaging
- Great for visual book presentations
- Strong BookTube community
- Content remains discoverable long-term

**Types of BookTube Channels:**

1. **Book Review Channels**
   - Reviews of new and classic books
   - Monthly book hauls
   - Reading vlogs

2. **Genre-Specific Channels**
   - Romance, fantasy, mystery, etc.
   - Deep dives into genre tropes
   - Community-focused

3. **Writing Advice Channels**
   - Writing tips and techniques
   - Publishing guidance
   - Author career advice

4. **Literary Analysis Channels**
   - Deep dives into themes
   - Critical analysis
   - Book discussions

5. **Author Interview Channels**
   - Conversations with authors
   - Behind-the-scenes content
   - Writing process discussions

### Finding BookTube Opportunities

**Search Methods:**
- YouTube search: "[Genre] book review"
- "[Genre] BookTube"
- "Author interview YouTube"
- Check video descriptions of similar authors
- Follow recommendation algorithm
- Join BookTube Facebook groups

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Subscriber count
- View counts per video
- Engagement (likes, comments)
- Upload frequency
- Content quality
- Genre alignment
- Review policy

### YouTube Outreach

**Outreach Template:**

```
Subject: Collaboration Opportunity: [Book Title] by [Your Name]

Hi [Channel Name],

I've been watching your channel and loved your recent video on [Specific Video]. Your analysis of [specific detail] was spot-on!

I'm [Your Name], author of [Book Title], a [genre] novel that I think would resonate with your audience. Here's why:

[Book Title] features [elements that match their channel focus]:
• [Element #1 that appeals to their audience]
• [Element #2]
• [Element #3]

I'd love to send you a review copy (digital or physical, your preference) for potential coverage on your channel. No obligation—only if it appeals to you and you think your audience would enjoy it.

I'm also happy to:
- Provide an author Q&A video
- Participate in a live chat
- Offer a giveaway for your subscribers
- Create exclusive content for your channel

You can learn more about me and the book at [your website].

Thanks for considering, and keep creating awesome content!

Best,
[Your Name]
[Email]
[Social Media Handles]
```

**Video Collaboration Ideas:**

1. **Book Review Video**
   - Send advance copy
   - They review honestly
   - You promote their video

2. **Author Interview**
   - Q&A format
   - Discuss book and process
   - Can be live or pre-recorded

3. **Book Unboxing**
   - Send special edition or signed copy
   - They unbox on camera
   - Great visual content

4. **Tag Videos**
   - "Books on My Shelf" tag
   - "Reading Challenge" participation
   - Genre-specific tags

5. **Giveaway Collaboration**
   - You provide books as prizes
   - They host the giveaway
   - Builds both audiences

6. **Writing Advice Video**
   - Share your expertise
   - Tips for aspiring authors
   - Behind-the-scenes of publishing

### Preparing for Video Interviews

**Technical Requirements:**
- Good camera (webcam or DSLR)
- Proper lighting (ring light recommended)
- Clear audio (external mic if possible)
- Clean, professional background
- Stable internet connection
- Zoom/Skype/StreamYard setup

**Visual Presentation:**
- Professional but approachable appearance
- Solid colored clothing (avoid patterns)
- Good posture and eye contact
- Engaging facial expressions
- Have book nearby to show
- Bookshelf background (if applicable)

**Content Preparation:**
- More visual/animated than podcast
- Prepare to show your book on camera
- Have key quotes ready to share
- Practice storytelling with gestures
- Prepare visuals if doing screen share
- Be camera-ready and enthusiastic

## Part 3: Traditional Media Outreach

### Television

**Local TV Opportunities:**
- Morning shows
- Local news programs
- Community spotlight segments
- Local talk shows
- Public access programming

**Why Local TV:**
- Accessible for most authors
- Community interest angle
- Builds local credibility
- Practice for bigger opportunities
- Can lead to book sales in local stores

**Pitch Angles for TV:**
- Local author angle
- Timely/seasonal tie-in
- Unique personal story
- Expertise on current events
- Community involvement
- Human interest story

**TV Outreach Process:**

1. **Research Shows**
   - Watch your target programs
   - Identify segment types
   - Note producer names
   - Understand their audience

2. **Create a TV-Ready Pitch**
   - Visual elements (your book is photogenic)
   - Clear, concise talking points
   - Why NOW (timeliness)
   - Local connection
   - Audience benefit

3. **Contact the Right Person**
   - Usually the segment producer
   - Sometimes assignment editor
   - Contact info usually on station website
   - Call during business hours

**TV Pitch Email Template:**

```
Subject: Local Author Story: [Compelling Hook]

Dear [Producer Name],

I'm a [City] author with a unique story that would interest your [Show Name] viewers.

My debut novel, [Book Title], [accomplishment or unique angle]. As a [local connection - resident, business owner, etc.], I believe this local-author-makes-good story would resonate with your audience.

Here's what makes this story timely and visual for TV:
• [Visual element or demonstration possibility]
• [Timely angle or news hook]
• [Human interest element]

I'm available for an in-studio interview or remote appearance and can provide:
- Engaging conversation about [topic]
- Visual elements (book, props, etc.)
- Local perspective on [relevant topic]

I've attached a one-sheet with more details. Would you be interested in featuring this story?

Thanks for considering!

[Your Name]
[Phone Number]
[Email]
[Website]
```

**TV Interview Tips:**
- Dress professionally (solid colors, avoid white)
- Speak in soundbites (10-15 seconds)
- Make eye contact with interviewer (not camera)
- Use gestures but don't fidget
- Hold your book naturally when appropriate
- Smile and be enthusiastic
- Practice your key messages
- Arrive early
- Bring multiple copies of book

### Radio

**Radio Opportunities:**
- Local NPR stations
- Commercial radio morning shows
- Talk radio programs
- Community radio
- Internet radio shows
- Sirius XM (for bigger authors)

**Why Radio:**
- Easier to book than TV
- Can do remotely
- Great for audio learners
- Less pressure than video
- Often longer interview time
- Strong local following

**Radio Pitch Approach:**

Similar to podcast but focus on:
- Local angle for local stations
- Timely news hooks
- Controversial or debate-worthy topics
- Expert commentary
- Caller interaction opportunities

**Radio Interview Best Practices:**
- Speak clearly and at good pace
- Vary your vocal tone
- Paint pictures with words
- Tell compelling stories
- Mention book title naturally
- Give call-to-action (where to buy)
- Provide station website URL

### Newspapers and Magazines

**Print Media Opportunities:**
- Local newspaper features
- Op-ed pieces
- Book reviews
- Author interviews
- Magazine articles
- Literary journals
- Trade publications

**Types of Newspaper Coverage:**

1. **Book Review**
   - Traditional review
   - Usually books editor
   - Advance copy required

2. **Feature Article**
   - Longer profile piece
   - Human interest angle
   - Lifestyle or arts section

3. **Op-Ed/Commentary**
   - You write the piece
   - Tied to current events
   - Establishes expertise

4. **News Tie-In**
   - Your expertise on breaking news
   - Book as supporting credential
   - Op-ed or quoted expert

5. **Event Coverage**
   - Book launch announcement
   - Speaking engagement
   - Community calendar listing

**Newspaper Pitch Template:**

```
Subject: Story Pitch: [Compelling Headline]

Dear [Editor Name],

I'm pitching a story about [compelling angle] that would interest [Publication] readers.

As a [local connection] and author of [Book Title], I bring a unique perspective on [topic]. My book addresses [relevant issue], which is particularly timely given [current event or trend].

Story angle: [Specific, newsworthy angle in one sentence]

This story would appeal to your readers because:
- [Reader benefit #1]
- [Local relevance]
- [Timeliness]

I can provide:
- Expert commentary
- Personal anecdotes
- Local perspective
- Photos and graphics

Would you be interested in covering this story or would you like me to write an op-ed on this topic?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Contact Information]
```

**Building Media Relationships:**
- Follow journalists on social media
- Comment on their articles
- Share their work
- Become a reliable source
- Respond quickly to inquiries
- Provide good quotes
- Meet deadlines
- Be available when needed

## Media Kit Essentials

### Components

1. **Author Bio**
   - Short version (50 words)
   - Medium version (150 words)
   - Long version (300+ words)

2. **High-Resolution Photos**
   - Professional headshot
   - Casual author photo
   - Book cover image
   - Action shots (writing, events)

3. **Book Information**
   - Title, subtitle, genre
   - Publication date and publisher
   - ISBN and formats available
   - Brief description (25, 50, 100 words)
   - Key themes and topics
   - Purchase links

4. **Sample Interview Questions**
   - 10-15 questions with answers
   - Mix of easy and deep questions
   - Include questions about your book
   - Questions about writing process
   - Questions about your expertise

5. **Media Mentions**
   - Previous appearances
   - Reviews and testimonials
   - Awards and recognition
   - Notable endorsements

6. **Contact Information**
   - Email and phone
   - Website and social media
   - Publicist (if you have one)
   - Availability

7. **Press Release**
   - Newsworthy announcement
   - Standard press release format
   - Contact information
   - High-resolution images

### Digital vs. Physical Media Kit

**Digital Kit:**
- PDF media kit
- Online press page
- Dropbox/Google Drive folder
- Email attachments
- Easy to update and distribute

**Physical Kit:**
- For in-person meetings
- Press folders with materials
- Business cards
- Book copies
- USB drive with digital assets

## Publicity Timeline

### 6-12 Months Before Release
- Create media kit
- Build media contact list
- Practice interview skills
- Hire publicist (if budget allows)

### 3-6 Months Before Release
- Begin outreach to long-lead publications
- Pitch feature stories
- Send advance copies to reviewers
- Schedule podcast interviews

### 1-3 Months Before Release
- Increase outreach frequency
- Schedule TV and radio appearances
- Place op-eds
- Confirm scheduled appearances

### Launch Month
- Peak media activity
- Daily social media promotion
- Follow up on all coverage
- Track all mentions

### Post-Launch
- Continue scheduled appearances
- Thank everyone who covered you
- Build relationships for next book
- Track and archive all coverage

## Conclusion

Media outreach requires persistence, professionalism, and patience. Start with accessible opportunities (podcasts, local media) and work your way up to larger platforms. Every appearance builds your experience, credibility, and audience. Remember that media relationships are long-term investments—treat producers, hosts, and journalists with respect, provide value to their audiences, and you'll create opportunities for years to come.

The key to successful media outreach is understanding that it's not about you or your book—it's about providing value to the media outlet's audience. Lead with how you'll help them, and the book promotion will follow naturally.
